## The headline finding

You came in calling yourself a builder, and the way you ask questions demonstrates it: leadership, your work, the thinking system you're using right now to ask these questions, the salon you want to convene. The audit confirmed it. It also surfaced a second pole, less consciously held — six of twelve questions are observer-mode, sense-making about categories you watch from outside rather than build inside. Your sense-making clusters in domains where you don't: AI markets you don't run, housing you don't build, nuclear you don't engineer, creative AI you watch with curiosity, products that evolve themselves. The match between *where you have practice* and *where you take the maker stance* is the structural finding of this report.

## Appendix — what changed this session

Four decisions, in order.

### Decision 1 — One steward question added

A new page pairs with [*"What does the AI moment ask of someone with my experience?"*](../wiki/questions/what-ai-moment-asks-of-my-experience.md). The existing question asks what the moment demands of someone who has spent twenty years building. The new one asks what about that practice is worth fighting to preserve.

> **New question:** [*"What about product creation practice should we fight to preserve as AI takes over the work?"*](../wiki/questions/product-creation-practice-worth-preserving-as-ai-takes-over.md)

The framing names what's at risk: pair-programming, code review, mentorship, the judgment that accumulates from making mistakes with real users. The inquiry is which of those the new tooling will retire before anyone notices they were load-bearing.

### Decision 2 — Leadership cluster collapsed from three to two

Three questions that read as peers turned out to be one question at three layers — interior disposition (*"How does a leader practice optimism when cynicism looks smarter in the room?"*), interpersonal practice ([*"How do you keep a team grounded when the pace of change never lets up?"*](../wiki/questions/keep-team-grounded-in-accelerating-change.md)), and structural artifact ([*"How do you avoid team misalignment with a constitutional roadmap as production accelerates?"*](../wiki/questions/team-alignment-with-roadmap-at-high-velocity.md)). The interior-disposition layer was the most absorbable. The optimism question is now retired; it survives as a sub-frame inside the grounding question.

### Decision 3 — Critic slot held open, not filled

Your reading already runs a critic seam: Project Deal on invisible inequality in agent-quality markets, Cutler and Caroli against cascade-thinking, Manikandan on the manager-as-ETL trap, Yegge's "embarrassing little kid's soccer team" line about the industry's CLI obsession. Three candidate questions surfaced. None was urgent enough. One would have eroded the existing roadmap-alignment question. The slot stays visible in the negative-space table, claimable when a sharper question emerges.

### Decision 4 — Four reframes, plus one v3 correction

One test for each maker question: *do you intend to act on this in the next year, or are you holding it at distance?*

| "How might a self-evolving product balance between what it wants to become and what its users want?" | "What patterns emerge in how self-evolving products choose between thesis and users?" | Not designing one — observing. |

| "What shifts when a person's learned AI travels with them across roles?" | *(kept)* | Already observer-form; v3 had it miscategorised. Framing tightened to drop the technical-artifact thread. |
| "How do we build modern-day salons that become engines of invention in an increasingly polarized world?" | *(kept; personal-stake line added)* | Aspirational maker — you want to convene one and don't yet know how. |

Stance distribution moved from **10/2/0/0** at the session's start to **6/5/0/1**, then to **5/6/0/1** after the post-retro correction in Decision 5 below. Every maker question that survived is somewhere you're actually building.

### Decision 5 — Post-retro correction: ai-for-acts reframed to observer

The end-of-session retro caught a miss in the Decision 4 audit. The page `ai-for-acts-of-radical-creativity` carried a "personal stake" line in its framing — *"Paul is training an AI on his own creative work"* — that was inaccurate; it had come through from elicitation and the audit trusted it without checking. In fact, the topic is held with curiosity rather than active practice. Page reframed: title kept (already observer-form: "How will AI be used…?"), framing rewritten to drop the inaccurate personal-stake claim and explicitly mark the relationship as curiosity-from-outside. Stance count: 6/5/0/1 → 5/6/0/1. Meta-finding for the skill: the audit cannot trust the page's existing framing as evidence of first-person practice; it must ask the user directly about each maker question, even when the framing already claims a personal stake.

## Appendix — the four stances

The set sorts across four intellectual stances. Each generates a different question form and accumulates a different kind of evidence.

### Builder / maker

You're designing, building, leading, or convening. The destination is action.

- **Question form:** "How do you build / lead / design X?"
- **What accumulates:** know-how about the practice of doing.
- **In your set:** five questions —

  - [*"How do you avoid team misalignment with a constitutional roadmap as production accelerates?"*](../wiki/questions/team-alignment-with-roadmap-at-high-velocity.md)

### Observer / sense-maker

You're watching a pattern unfold and trying to understand it. The destination is interpretation.

- **Question form:** "What is X actually doing? What patterns emerge?"
- **What accumulates:** evidence about how something works in the world.
- **In your set:** six questions —
  - [*"What patterns emerge as AI markets experiment with outcome-pricing instead of tokens?"*](../wiki/questions/ai-marketplace-paid-for-outcomes-not-tokens.md)
  - [*"What shifts when a person's learned AI travels with them across roles?"*](../wiki/questions/learned-ai-travels-with-person-across-roles.md)

  - [*"What happens to the world economy if personal-scale nuclear displaces oil?"*](../wiki/questions/personal-scale-nuclear-displaces-oil.md)
  - [*"How can we make contemporary housing as exciting and economical as mid-century modern was to build?"*](../wiki/questions/contemporary-housing-exciting-and-economical.md)

### Critic / skeptic

You're interrogating a consensus and arguing it's wrong. The destination is a sharpened argument.

- **Question form:** "Why is X assumed? What's wrong with the popular answer?"
- **What accumulates:** the case against a prevailing view.
- **In your set:** *none* — slot held open (Decision 3). Raw material for at least one is already in your reading.

### Steward / preserver

You're asking what's at risk of being lost and what you'd fight to keep. The destination is a defensible claim about what to preserve.

- **Question form:** "What about X is at risk? What's worth preserving?"
- **What accumulates:** the case for what to defend.
- **In your set:** one question —
  - [*"What about product creation practice should we fight to preserve as AI takes over the work?"*](../wiki/questions/product-creation-practice-worth-preserving-as-ai-takes-over.md)
